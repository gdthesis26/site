RESIST: Graphic Design Senior Show

ANNY LONG

FOCUS as resistance

Installation

Two-part display: The front podium will showcase a series of phone cases for both the Android Z Flip 7 and the iPhone 17, or the latest iPhone model most people have. Behind the podium, there’s an iPad hanging that plays a looping commercial promoting the need for, and use of, the cases, along with a poster description explaining the brand’s name and how it relates to the smartphone case concept.

Project

The word resist can mean different things to different people—it can feel political, or it can be literal. For me, I wanted to explore what it means to "resist" in daily life—specifically, our relationship with our devices and the constant pull our phones are designed to have on our attention and its causing many of us tech fatigue. Right now, the solution is to turn the phone off or walk away. Some people switch to an older phone—like a 2000s Nokia—or try to "quit" altogether. Despite these efforts we still can't fully unplug. The convenience of being reachable—for friends or work—keeps pulling us back in. Since our lives are intertwined with the digital world, I wanted to propose instead of fighting the phone, I decided we need to work with it. I'm taking a meta-concept approach: using the phone to protect us from the phone. The solution I created is called Chrysalis—an app accessed through a smartphone case. After downloading the app, you attach the case, turn it on then the case will locks the phone and triggers the Chrysalis app to take over your home screen, showing only the timer interface.

You set a timer for how long you want to disconnect; during that time, you stay in a "chrysalis" state, so you're less tempted to switch to other apps. Chrysalis also offers four types of audio accompaniment to support the pause and help you stay in your workflow. When the timer ends, the smart case unlocks, returning the phone to you, and the case becomes a normal phone case. My design theory was to create a simple accessory you can integrate into your lifestyle—easy to use, and focused on doing one thing well. I also thought about the space you enter as the screen portal changes. I chose a soft blue-purple gradient and other calm colors to create a meditative environment, using the butterfly's chrysalis phase as inspiration for the timer symbol.

I chose sound because my research showed that when we step away from the screen, we still need a sensory replacement—something that can calm us.

Biography

Anny Long is a San Francisco-based interdisciplinary designer and a CCA senior earning a BFA in Communication Design.
